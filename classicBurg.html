HOW TO CHEESE A BURG!

Ingredients

Patties:

  • 1 lb (450 g) ground beef (80% lean, 20% fat for juiciness)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp garlic powder (optional)
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce (optional)

Toppings:

  • 4 burger buns (brioche or sesame preferred)
  • 4 slices of cheese (American, cheddar, or your favorite)
  • Lettuce leaves
  • Tomato slices
  • Pickles (optional)
  • Onion slices (raw or caramelized)
  • Condiments: ketchup, mustard, mayonnaise, etc.

Steps

  1. Form the patties:

    Divide the ground beef into 4 equal portions (about 4 oz each).

    Gently shape into round patties, about ¾ inch thick.

    Press a small dent in the center of each patty with your thumb (this helps them cook evenly).

    Season both sides with salt, pepper, and garlic powder.

  2. Cook the patties:

    Heat a grill or skillet over medium-high heat.

    Add a bit of oil if using a skillet.

    Cook patties for 3-4 minutes per side for medium doneness.

    In the last minute, place a slice of cheese on each patty and cover (to melt it nicely).

  3. Toast the buns:

    While the burgers cook, lightly toast your buns on the grill or in a pan until golden.

  4. Assemble your burger:

    Spread sauces on both buns.

    Add lettuce, tomato, and onion to the bottom bun.

    Place the cheesy patty on top.

    Add pickles if you like, then close with the top bun.

  5. Serve hot!

    Pair with fries, onion rings, or a cold drink.
